Explore Mental Health

Understanding mental health and recognizing symptoms is a step towards better well-being.

What is Mental Health?

Mental health encompasses emotional, psychological, and social well-being. It affects how we think, feel, and act. It also determines how we handle stress, relate to others, and make choices.

Common Mental Health Disorders and Their Symptoms

Anxiety Disorders

Anxiety disorders involve excessive fear or worry. Symptoms may include:

  • Restlessness or feeling on edge
  • Fatigue
  • Difficulty concentrating
  • Rapid heartbeat

Depression

Depression is characterized by persistent sadness and a lack of interest or pleasure in activities. Symptoms may include:

  • Feelings of hopelessness
  • Loss of energy
  • Difficulty sleeping or oversleeping
  • Changes in appetite

Bipolar Disorder

Bipolar disorder involves extreme mood swings, including emotional highs (mania or hypomania) and lows (depression). Symptoms may include:

  • Increased energy or activity during mania
  • Impulsivity
  • Feelings of sadness during depressive episodes
  • Difficulty maintaining focus

Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD)

PTSD can develop after exposure to a traumatic event. Symptoms may include:

  • Flashbacks or intrusive memories
  • Nightmares
  • Severe emotional distress
  • Difficulty concentrating

Eating Disorders

Eating disorders involve unhealthy preoccupations with food, weight, or body shape. Symptoms may include:

  • Restricting food intake
  • Binge eating
  • Excessive concern with body image
  • Physical health issues like fatigue

Seeking Help

If you or someone you know is experiencing symptoms of a mental health disorder, reaching out for professional help is essential. Early intervention can make a significant difference.
